Estonia (Eesti in Estonian - a Finnic language) is a Northern European country. It was the 48th nation to join the Hattrick World.

The Republic of Estonia (Eesti Vabariik) has a population of 1.3 million people. Tallinn, the capital of Estonia, and Tartu are largest cities.
A distinct Estonian national identity began to emerge in the early 20th century, culminating in independence from Russia in 1920. After World War II, Soviets re-occupation established the Estonian SSR. In 1987 the peaceful Singing Revolution began against Soviet rule, resulting in the restoration of de facto independence on 20 August 1991.

Emin Muhammet Oğulbay is a retired Belgian football player, who played his full career for Lokomotiv Veltem Back-on-Track in Belgium. He frequently played as a winger, although he has appeared as inner midfielder and also showed defending skills. On top of that, he became topscorer during (global) season 38 in the Belgian IX.24 series with 11 goals and was part of the team that played a perfect season (42 points) and promoted to eighth division that season.

Biography

Emin was born and raised in West Philadelphia where he spent most of his days on playgrounds being mostly occupied with chillin', relaxin' and shootin' b-ball outside of school. Suddenly however, his life got flipped upside down as a couple of guys, seemingly up to no good, made trouble. After he got in a little fight his mom got scared and sent him over to the little town of Veltem to live with his aunt and uncle.

He took a "Fresh" cab, which are rare to find, and left for Veltem. After a long trip he arrived there, got a new hobby in football as he joined Lokomotiv Veltem Back-on-Track and soon forgot about his parents. Then he took his place on the throne as "Prince of Veltem" where stayed for almost 20 seasons, before retiring to become a sports journalist.

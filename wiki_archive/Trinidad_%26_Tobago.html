Trinidad & Tobago (also called T&T in formally) is an North American country. It was the 93rd nation to join the Hattrick World.

The Republic of Trinidad & Tobago is the southernmost island country in the Caribbean with 1.3 million inhabitants. The economy is primarily industrial; much of the nation's wealth is derived from its large reserves of oil and natural gas.
A colony of the Spanish Empire following the arrival of Christopher Columbus in 1498, the islands changed hands among Spanish, British, French, Dutch and Courlander colonisers before becaming Britain in 1802. Trinidad and Tobago obtained independence in 1962, becoming a republic in 1976.
